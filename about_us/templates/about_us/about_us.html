Our Story

Island Rescue Organization, or more commonly known as IRO, is a non-profit, non-government organization dedicated to helping the plight of animals in the region through Rescue, Education, and Advocacy. Through these 3 pillars, the organization has established partnerships among communities, the private sector, and LGUs through volunteerism in the last decade.

Founded in 2010 by a group of animal advocates and pet lovers in the island, IRO has evolved from fostering rescued animals to re-homing them into loving families. Over the years, the team has been innovating ways to promote long-term solutions to lessen the population of stray dogs and cats, and even elevate the quality of life of those who remain on the streets.

Guba where the bulk and general populace of the rescued animals are being housed.

With over 100 animals at its rescue centers and reliant on sponsorships and donations, the organization mounts several projects annually to raise funds that cover food costs, veterinary care, and overall operations. The team utilizes its education campaigns to continue to engage the general public in the mission, all while working with several different sectors of society.

IRO aims to have a society where animal rescue organizations are no longer needed, and where all living beings are treated humanely, recognizing that humans and animals are interconnected and important for the survival of our environment and planet.
