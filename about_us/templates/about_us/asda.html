Island Rescue Organization

Compassion in Action for Animals in Need

About Us

The Island Rescue Organization (IRO) is a non-profit and non-government organization committed to the rescue, care, and advocacy for animals in the region. Founded in 2010, IRO has become a beacon of hope for stray and abandoned animals through its core pillars: Rescue, Education, and Advocacy.

Over the past decade, IRO has built a strong foundation of community partnerships, engaging volunteers, private sector organizations, and local government units (LGUs) in their mission to create a safe haven for animals.

Our Shelters

IRO currently operates three dedicated shelters that provide a safe and nurturing environment for animals in need:

  • Barili Shelter: A sanctuary for senior and special-needs dogs.
  • Mandaue Shelter: A space designed for cats, including those with special medical needs.
  • Cebu City Shelter (Brgy. Guba): The primary shelter housing the majority of rescued animals.

These shelters are run solely by volunteers, and each plays a vital role in improving the lives of animals while preparing them for rehoming or ensuring they are cared for throughout their lives.

Our Impact

With over 100 animals currently under its care, IRO relies on the generosity of sponsors and donors to cover essential costs, including food, veterinary care, and day-to-day operations. Annual projects and fundraising initiatives also enable IRO to sustain its mission while spreading awareness about responsible pet ownership and animal welfare.

The organization continues to advocate for long-term solutions, such as spaying and neutering programs, to address the growing stray animal population while improving their quality of life.

Our Vision

To create a society where animal rescue organizations are no longer needed, and all living beings are treated with dignity and respect. IRO envisions a world where humans and animals coexist in harmony, fostering a sustainable and compassionate environment for future generations.
